Our Story

We grew up on opposite sides of the world but found each other somewhere in between...

Two adventurous Canadian & Australian souls wandered to London, one in 2008 and one in 2010 to live, travel and explore the world. Unexpectedly meeting in 2011 through mutual friends, Michael & Adeline had an instant connection - enjoying each other's sense of humour, spending time cooking together and recreating their parents' home cooked meals, running in Regents Park to the Regents Canal and seeing new parts of the world together. Time passed and their relationship strengthened, with a realisation that though they had grown up on opposite sides of the world in Northern & Southern hemispheres, they shared common values and similar aspirations in life. Two years later in 2013, Michael proposed to Adeline sky high at the Shard in London. In 2014 they begin the rest of their lives together and celebrate their love in the places where they were taught how to love by their own families - in Australia and Canada.

Sydney

Thank you to our family & friends from around the world from Sydney, London, New York, Toronto, Paris, Toulouse, Singapore, Johor Baru and Penang. We were truly blessed to have so many of our loved ones gathered to witness the official celebration of our mutual commitment, unity & love.

15th March 2014 It was a sunny and calm Saturday morning, with Adeline getting ready with her bridesmaids Christine & Nathalie at her family home on the North Shore and Mike and his groomsmen Dan & Matt preparing for the day ahead in an apartment nearby - with suits, shiny shoes and glass of whiskey in hand to calm the nerves. The wedding ceremony - intimate, teary and personal, took place at St Francis Xavier Catholic church in Lavender Bay and was celebrated by Fr John Hodgson - the parish priest from Adeline's childhood days. To commemorate Adeline's Chinese culture, family and close friends gathered back at her home for a traditional tea ceremony and buffet lunch. The bridal party were then whisked away by limousine to the serene and magical McKell Park in Darling Point for wedding photos. The celebrations & fun continued at Zest Waterfront in Point Piper with a slight moment of rain, followed by rainbows and picturesque views of the Sydney harbour. It is hard to describe the overwhelming feeling of seeing the efforts of months of planning and organisation finally come together! Thank you to our family & friends for your presence and kind words words of support - we will always remember our wedding day with fond memories and we are both extremely grateful that you were part of this special moment in our lives!

Toronto

Double wedding happiness, twice the fun! We were lucky and fortunate to celebrate our love for the second time in Toronto with family and friends from around Canada, the USA, UK and Australia joining us for Wedding 2.0.

31st May 2014 Wedding's in Michael's extended family are indeed a true love story. Aunties and uncles come together months before the big day to plan and organize. For this particular wedding occasion, there was a DIY backyard marquee, beautiful handmade flowers and decorations, delicious Vietnamese cuisine for lunch and who can't forget Michael's favourite suckling pig - it is amazing to witness families unite with 'all hands on deck'. To pay tribute to Michael's Vietnamese heritage and culture, a special tea ceremony was held in his Aunty Mai & Uncle Khai's house where tea was served as a sign of respect and gratitude to Michael's grandfather, parents, aunts & uncles and Adeline's parents. Michael's cousins also came together to give their well wishes and words of wisdom.



Post the sumptuous feast, the happy couple took some photos in the ethereal James Gardens in nearby Etobicoke. More close family and friends joined in the wedding dinner celebration at Sassafraz in Toronto, with speeches from the bride & groom's fathers, Michael's primary school friend Manoj and Adeline's Sydney friend Linda. Michael also said a few words to express his thanks to everyone for their generosity & sharing in the start of the newlywed's life together. The night ended with dance floor fun and many of Michael's family members posing with the wedding cake to reminisce about their own nuptials! With sincere appreciation, thank you to everyone who brought joy to our day and happiness to our lives. Love & blessings!
